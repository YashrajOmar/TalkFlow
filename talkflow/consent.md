# TalkFlow Consent & Recording Policy

Recording conversations with multiple participants introduces important legal and ethical requirements. Before using **Mode 2: Full Interview Mode**, you must read and agree to this policy.

---

## ⚖️ Wiretapping & Recording Laws

Voice recording is regulated by state, federal, and international wiretapping and privacy laws. These generally fall into two categories:

1.  **One-Party Consent:** In these jurisdictions (e.g., US Federal law, and US states like New York and Texas), you can record a conversation if at least one participant (you) agrees to the recording.
2.  **All-Party Consent (Two-Party Consent):** In these jurisdictions (e.g., US states like California, Florida, Illinois, Pennsylvania, and countries like the United Kingdom, Canada, and EU member states), **everyone** participating in the conversation must explicitly agree to be recorded.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Because you may be interviewing with companies or individuals located in all-party consent states or countries, you should **always** request explicit permission before capturing interviewer audio. Recording without consent in these regions can carry civil and criminal liabilities.

---

## 💬 Template Scripts for Requesting Consent

You can use these templates to ask for permission at the start of your interview or practice session:

### Option A: Spoken Consent Request (Verbal)
> *"Hi [Interviewer Name], I am currently practicing to improve my English grammar and interview delivery. I use a local-first speech trainer application that transcribes my answers on my machine and suggests grammar improvements. It processes everything offline and does not share my data. Would you mind if I recorded the audio of this session for my private practice?"*

### Option B: Chat Consent Request (Google Meet, Zoom, MS Teams chat)
> *"Hi [Interviewer Name], I'm using a local-first, privacy-safe speech coach that logs my answers on my machine to give me feedback on my English grammar and sentence structure after our session. It runs entirely locally and does not upload data. Would it be alright if I enabled it for our conversation today? Thank you!"*

---

## 🛑 Platform & Interview Site Rules
*   **Company Policy:** Many companies prohibit recording interview questions, coding assessments, or case studies to protect their intellectual property. If a company declines your recording request, immediately switch to **Mode 1: Self Recording** (which captures only your microphone input).
*   **Third-Party Services:** Always respect the terms of service of mock interview networks or tutoring portals when recording.
